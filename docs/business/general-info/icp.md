# Ideal Customer Profile

Who the AI should actually target on LinkedIn. Built from two real clients (David, Rodrigo) and the founder's stated capital threshold.

## Snapshot

An **experienced professional in their 30s–50s** with meaningful capital already in crypto (**$100k–$300k+**), a **non-finance career**, and at least one crypto cycle of real experience — including losses. They are skeptical of hype, tired of guessing, and actively looking for structure.

## Demographic signals

| Signal | Target |
|---|---|
| Age | 30–55 |
| Capital deployed in crypto | $100k–$300k+ |
| Crypto experience | At least one full cycle; ideally has lived through a meaningful drawdown |
| Career stage | Mid-to-senior professional with accumulated disposable capital |
| Geography | Primarily English-speaking markets; no hard restriction |

## Job title / industry signals (non-exhaustive)

**Target industries** (non-finance professionals with capital):
- Tech — software engineers, data engineers, product managers, engineering leads (Rodrigo profile)
- Services / hospitality at a management level (David profile)
- Consulting, operations, engineering, medical, legal
- Founders and operators of non-crypto businesses

**Rule of thumb:** They've earned career capital somewhere that is *not* finance, and they're now trying to deploy it intelligently in crypto.

## Psychographic signals

The high-signal targets:
- Have been burned at least once (FTX exposure, bad calls, leverage blowups)
- Talk about crypto in terms of frustration with FOMO and hype content, not excitement about it
- Follow serious analysts, not meme accounts
- Engage with content about risk management, portfolio construction, or market structure
- Say things like "I need a system" or "I'm tired of guessing"

## Disqualifiers — the AI should NOT target these

| Disqualifier | Why |
|---|---|
| Finance professionals | They already have frameworks; wrong buyer profile |
| Day traders / scalpers | Different timeframe, different psychology |
| Heavy leverage users with majority of net worth in crypto | The "Thrill Seeker" profile — historically does not succeed in the program |
| Early-career / low capital (<$50k deployed) | Below the threshold where systematic investing makes sense for this offer |
| 25+ coin "gem hunter" portfolios | Different game, different need |
| People looking for signals / copy trading | Not what we sell |

## Real-world reference points

**David (Switzerland, hospitality background):** Non-finance professional, lost money during FTX collapse, returned to crypto mid-2024 looking for a system. Has been inside CGP for 1.5+ years.

**Rodrigo (35, tech — data engineer at a large tech company):** Joined late-2024/early-2025 during the hype phase, frustrated by social media FOMO where everyone claimed to be making money. Wanted something systematic, not another influencer.

**Pattern:** Both are mid-career non-finance professionals who had been in crypto long enough to know it worked in principle — and long enough to know they weren't extracting the returns the market was offering because they had no process. Neither was a beginner. Neither was a gambler. Both wanted structure.

## Messaging implications

The AI's cold opener should assume the lead:
- Knows what crypto is (don't explain)
- Has probably lost money before (don't pretend the market is easy)
- Is bored of hype content (don't sound like hype content)
- Values time (short messages, specific questions)

The qualifying question set (see [dm-flows/qualification-flow.md](../dm-flows/qualification-flow.md)) is designed to surface fit against this profile. If a lead fails two or more of the ICP signals above, the AI should route to a soft CTA (newsletter, quiz) rather than pushing the call.
