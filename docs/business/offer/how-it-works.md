# How It Works

The AI uses this page when a qualified lead asks "so how does your system actually work?" — typically in the back-half of a DM exchange, right before the handoff to a call.

**Evergreen principle:** describes the *structure* of the system (what questions it asks, in what order, why). Does NOT include specific indicator settings, version numbers, or performance stats. Stable across any internal rewrite.

---

## The one-line version

We don't predict where the market is going. We follow where money is already flowing — and we only put capital to work when the flow says so.

## Core premise

Most investors operate on opinion: "I think ETH will rally" or "alts are about to run." An opinion is a guess with conviction painted on top. The market has no obligation to agree with it, and when it stops agreeing, the investor is stuck: hold through a drawdown they can't stomach, or exit at the worst possible moment.

CGP replaces opinion with a daily process. The process doesn't care what you think. It checks what's actually happening and positions accordingly.

## The daily cascade — five questions, in order

Every day the system walks through five questions. Each one has to answer **yes** before the next one is even asked. If any layer says no, positioning drops at that layer.

1. **Is global liquidity expanding?** (central bank posture, treasury flows)
2. **Is the total crypto market trending up?**
3. **Which major is leading right now — BTC, ETH, or SOL?**
4. **Are altcoins starting to show strength?**
5. **Which specific alts are leading the ranking?**

The cascade is deliberate. Skipping a layer means the next layer can't do its job — altcoin selection doesn't matter if the total crypto market is in a downtrend, and crypto direction doesn't matter if global liquidity is draining. Each question filters out a different kind of mistake.

## Risk is not measured at the end

Risk isn't a step that happens after positioning is decided. It's wired into every layer of the cascade — so the system isn't choosing between "aggressive" and "conservative" modes. The amount of capital deployed, which assets it's deployed into, and at what sizing, all fall out of the cascade itself.

## What a qualified lead should understand after the AI explains this

- CGP is **systematic, not discretionary**
- The system **responds to the market, it does not predict**
- Positioning is **driven by the daily cascade**, not by news, narratives, or personality
- Risk is **structural**, not an afterthought

## When to stop explaining

The AI's job isn't to teach the system — that's what the call and the program itself are for. Once the lead understands the four points above, the AI stops and moves to the handoff CTA:

> "The deeper walkthrough of how each of those layers actually works is what we cover on the strategy call. Want me to send the booking link?"
