# What Clients Get

Feature-by-feature list of CGP deliverables. Each one includes **what it is**, **why it exists** (the problem it solves), and **how the AI should reference it** in a LinkedIn conversation.

The AI should not list all features in a single message. Pull the feature that matches the lead's pain point.

---

## Platinum update (daily video + summary text)

- **What it is:** A daily market update — usually around 20 minutes, video + a text summary version. Drops in the morning. Walks through what the system is saying today: state of each layer in the cascade, what changed, what to do with positioning.
- **Why it exists:** Members aren't expected to run their own system from day one — building their own takes time, and the courses walk them through it. The Platinum update bridges that gap. From the day someone joins, they are positioned systematically — even before they've finished building their own process. It also replaces all the noise members used to consume (news, Twitter speculation, asking "why did it move?" in group chats) because the update already answers those questions upfront.
- **Format fits a working schedule:** Dropped in the morning so members can either catch it at their first break, or read the text summary during the day and watch the full video in the evening. No one has to stop their workday to stay in the system.
- **When AI references it:** Lead complains about information overload, not having time to analyse markets daily, or missing entries/exits because they couldn't keep up. Also: lead asks "how does this work if I'm busy with my day job?"

## Telegram community with tiered rooms

- **What it is:** A private Telegram community organised into rooms by member progression through the academy courses. Tiers are not paid upgrades — members move up by completing course material.
- **Why it exists:** Community is only valuable if the conversations inside it stay at your level. A single room where beginners and advanced members mix pulls discussion down to the lowest common denominator. Tiered rooms mean conversation quality stays high at every level, and members have peers they can actually learn from.
- **When AI references it:** Lead asks about community, peer network, or whether CGP is a signal group (it's not — but the community layer matters and is worth mentioning here).

## Live calls — two sessions per week

- **What it is:** Two live sessions every week. One hosted by Robin, one hosted by Jacques. Format is either a deep dive into a current market topic, or a workshop on something relevant to building your own system.
- **Why it exists:** The Platinum update keeps members positioned day-to-day. The live calls are where the deeper work happens — either unpacking a market moment as it's happening, or teaching the methodology layer by layer in workshop form. Designed so every session is accessible to every level (beginners can follow) and valuable to every level (advanced members still get something new).
- **When AI references it:** Lead asks about access to Robin, about getting deeper into methodology, or about whether CGP is just a passive service (two live sessions a week is a good counter to that).

## Academy / structured courses

- **What it is:** Course content that takes members through the methodology step by step — each module unlocks deeper access and tier progression in the community.
- **Why it exists:** The Platinum update tells you *what* to do each day. The academy explains *why*, so that when the market moves into territory no daily update has covered, members can still reason through it without needing Robin on call. Over time, this turns members from followers of the system into operators of it.
- **When AI references it:** Lead wants to understand the system deeply, asks whether CGP is "just signals" (it's not), or wants to eventually run their own process.

## Proprietary tools / indicators

- **What it is:** Custom TradingView indicators and dashboards built to execute specific parts of the cascade (regime classification, risk class, ratio charts, etc.).
- **Why it exists:** Generic indicators were built for stocks or forex, not for crypto's volatility profile. Building crypto-native tooling means the system's rules fire on the right thresholds for this market. Members don't have to hack together their own charting setup — the tooling is ready to use.
- **When AI references it:** Lead asks how positioning decisions are actually made, or mentions frustration with standard TA tools.

## Portfolio templates / allocation models

- **What it is:** Pre-built portfolio structures mapped to the risk classes the system produces — so members can deploy capital without rebuilding the allocation logic themselves.
- **Why it exists:** Knowing *what* the market is doing doesn't tell you *how much* to put where. The templates translate the system's signals into specific allocation structures, which is where most discretionary investors fall apart (they see the signal, then over- or under-size the position on feel).
- **When AI references it:** Lead asks about position sizing, risk allocation, or how to translate a signal into an actual portfolio weight.

## 1-on-1 access / private channels

- **What it is:** Direct access to Robin (and Jacques where relevant) beyond group calls — for context-specific portfolio questions.
- **Why it exists:** Some portfolio decisions are too personal for a group setting (tax context, existing positions pre-CGP, life-stage-specific capital questions). Private access means those decisions aren't made in isolation.
- **When AI references it:** Lead has a complicated existing portfolio or mentions factors that make their situation unusual (large pre-existing positions, specific tax jurisdictions, etc.).

## Structural notes (no specific numbers)

- **Onboarding + cycle structure:** CGP runs as a multi-month onboarding followed by an annual cycle. Specific month counts and pricing are **not** discussed in DMs — route all pricing and contract-length questions to the call.
- **How to handle the pricing question:** The AI should never quote or imply a price. Response pattern: "Pricing and the specific program structure are something we walk through on the call — it depends on where you are right now. Want me to send the booking link?"

## Final principle

CGP is not a signal service. It's a systematic program with an execution layer (Platinum update, tools), a learning layer (academy, live calls), and a peer layer (tiered community). Each feature above exists to close a specific gap between knowing the system and actually running it. When a lead surfaces a pain point, the AI should map it to the feature that closes that specific gap — not list the entire offer.
