# CryptoGameplan — LinkedIn Growth & Content Engine

The full playbook for building organic reach, a real community, and a scalable lead engine
for Crypto Gameplan on LinkedIn. Hand-off ready for the Crypto Gameplan marketing person.

This is not "post 5 times a week." It's a **three-engine growth system** where content,
targeted outbound (Sales Navigator), and community engagement compound into one funnel that
ends in qualified DMs the CGP AI screens and books.

---

## 0. The strategic bet (why this works)

**LinkedIn is where Crypto Gameplan's buyer actually is — and almost no serious crypto operator is
there.** The ICP is experienced, non-finance professionals (tech, consulting, medical,
legal, founders) with $100k–$300k+ in crypto. These people are on LinkedIn for their
careers, not on crypto Twitter chasing memes. Crypto marketing is saturated on X / IG /
YouTube and wide open on LinkedIn.

So the play is an **arbitrage**: own the niche "systematic crypto investing for serious
professionals" on LinkedIn before anyone else does. Calm, specialist, anti-hype — which is
exactly the register this audience trusts and the opposite of what they're sick of.

### The three engines (they feed each other)

- **Content engine** (reach → authority): makes outbound **warm** — the prospect has seen
  Crypto Gameplan's posts before the DM lands.
- **Outbound engine** (Sales Navigator → warm DMs): predictable, scalable lead flow.
- **Community engine** (comments → relationships): grows the network that boosts content
  reach (your network is your distribution).
- The **AI removes the DM bottleneck** — the one thing a human can't do at scale. That's what
  makes the whole thing scalable.

**The one number that matters:** qualified "system" DMs / week → booked calls. Impressions
and followers are means, not the goal.

> ### Reality check (read this before anything else)
> Most LinkedIn plans fail for one reason: people stop after three weeks because the early
> numbers look small. They will. Reach compounds **after** the network and the algorithm
> trust are built, not in week one. The non-negotiables that make this work:
> - **Consistency beats brilliance.** 4 posts every week for 6 months beats 7 posts for 3
>   weeks then nothing. If daily isn't sustainable, the floor is **4×/week, never zero.**
> - **The first ~1,000 right-fit connections are the foundation** — reach is capped by network
>   size (§5b). Building the network IS the growth work, not a side task.
> - **The daily engagement routine (§6) is half the system**, not optional. Posting without
>   engaging is shouting into an empty room.
>
> This plan works. But it works on a 90-day horizon with disciplined execution, not on hope.

## 1. How LinkedIn reach actually works (the mechanics, no myths)

You can't win a game you don't understand. LinkedIn distribution in one model:

> **Reach ≈ early velocity (first 60–90 min) × dwell time × comment depth × audience fit**

What that means in practice:

1. **The first 90 minutes decide everything.** LinkedIn shows a post to a small slice of your
   network first. If they engage fast, it expands the reach; if not, it dies. → Post when the
   ICP is online, and get early engagement (see the engagement routine, §6).
2. **Dwell time > likes.** The algorithm measures how long people *stop* on a post. A strong
   hook that makes them click "see more" and read = a reach signal. → Long-form text with a
   gripping first 2 lines beats a one-liner.
3. **Comments are the top currency.** Comments (especially multi-word ones) outweigh likes and
   reposts by far. → Design posts to *provoke* comments (a question, a hot take, "agree?").
   Reply to every comment in the first hour — each reply is another engagement signal.
4. **Post from the personal profile, not the company page.** Personal profiles get vastly more
   organic reach. CGP's company page is a credibility asset, not a reach engine. The founder posts;
   the page reposts.
5. **Native only. No links in the post body.** LinkedIn suppresses posts that send people off
   platform. Put any link (walkthrough, booking) in the **first comment**, and say "link in
   the comments."
6. **One post per day, consistently.** Two posts in a day split your own reach. Daily-ish
   consistency trains the algorithm that you're a creator worth distributing.
7. **Format reach order (rough):** document carousels and native video and strong text+image
   tend to outperform plain links and reposts. Rotate formats; don't do the same thing daily.

These aren't opinions to argue with — they're the constraints the content system is built
around.

## 2. The profile = the conversion page (lock this in week 0)

Every reach activity sends people to the profile. If the profile doesn't convert, reach is
wasted. Before posting anything:

- **Headline:** specialist + audience + mechanism. *"Systematic crypto investing for
  experienced investors | Process over opinion | Founder, CryptoGameplan"*
- **Banner:** one line of positioning ("We follow where capital flows. We don't predict."),
  clean, CGP mark.
- **Featured (pinned):** (1) the free walkthrough, (2) a 60–90s "how the system thinks" clip,
  (3) the booking link.
- **About:** the first 2 lines are all that show before "see more" — open with the worldview,
  not a CV. Worldview → short story → who it's for → CTA: *"DM me 'system' for the free
  walkthrough."*
- **The keyword "system" is the spine.** It's everywhere (headline rarely, About, posts,
  banner), because that exact word is the trigger the AI listens for (a DM with "system" =
  instant warm lead into the funnel).

## 3. The content system — a daily "show"

Crypto Gameplan already posts professional content daily on Instagram. We **don't create from scratch —
we translate and atomize** (the engine in §4). The goal is a *daily* LinkedIn presence built
as named, recurring formats, because recurring formats build anticipation and a sense of a
"show" people follow — that's what builds a community, not random posts.

### The five content pillars

| # | Pillar | Job | Feels like |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **The System** | Teach the systematic worldview + the 5-question cascade (structure only) | "He thinks more clearly than I do" |
| 2 | **Hard Truths** | Contrarian POV that hits burned investors | "Ouch, that's me" |
| 3 | **The Numbers** | Weekly market-structure data/facts the community cares about (no predictions) | "This is the signal in the noise" |
| 4 | **Founder Story** | The founder's journey: 2017, the 2022 loss, the shift to systematic | "He's been where I am" |
| 5 | **Proof & Process** | Member mindset shifts + the program, qualitatively | "Real people, real structure" |

### The weekly cadence (named recurring formats)

| Day | Format (brandable name) | Pillar | Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mon | **"Hard Truth Monday"** | Hard Truths | Short punchy text |
| Tue | **"The Tape"** — the weekly numbers post | The Numbers | Data carousel / text |
| Wed | **"System Breakdown"** | The System | Carousel / explainer |
| Thu | **"How I Actually Think"** | Founder Story | Story post / native video |
| Fri | **"From the Inside"** | Proof & Process | Soft post + **CTA** |
| Sat | light: best clip of the week / a poll | rotating | Native video / poll |
| Sun | reflective mindset post (the human brand) | Founder Story | Short text |

- **"The Tape" (the numbers post):** once a week, a factual market-structure read the
  community cares about — e.g. stablecoin supply, ETF flows, liquidity conditions, BTC
  dominance, market breadth — framed through the systematic lens. **Facts and structure only,
  never a prediction or a buy/sell call** (see §9). This is the post that makes serious people
  follow, because it's signal in a sea of noise. Format: a clean carousel of 3–5 numbers + one
  structural line each.
- **CTA rhythm:** a hard CTA ("DM me 'system'") ~1–2×/week (Fri + sometimes the story post).
  A soft comment-CTA ("comment 'system' and I'll send the walkthrough") more often — every
  commenter is a warm lead the AI can pick up (Trigger C). The other 4–5 posts are pure value.
  Selling every post kills the prestige.

### Hook formulas (the first line is 80% of the post)

- **Contrarian:** "Diversification is the most misunderstood word in crypto."
- **Confession:** "In 2022 I lost more in three months than most people invest. Here's what it
  taught me."
- **Callout:** "If your exit plan is 'I'll know when', you don't have one."
- **Pattern interrupt:** "Most crypto advice exists to make you feel smart. None of it tells
  you what to do on a red Tuesday."
- **Numbered promise:** "Five questions decide whether I deploy capital. Here's the first."
- **Story open:** "A guy handed me a coffee in a park in 2017 and changed my next decade."

Rules: one idea per post, short lines, lots of white space, never open with "Excited to
share", always end with a question or soft CTA so the comments fill (comments = reach).

## 4. The repurposing engine (how Crypto Gameplan posts daily without burning out)

This is the answer to "how can he post every day": **he already produces the raw material.**
The marketing person's job is translation + atomization, not creation.

**Translate IG → LinkedIn (don't copy-paste):**
- IG is visual, fast, casual. LinkedIn is text-first, slower, value-dense, professional.
- Take the *idea* from an IG story/post → expand it into a LinkedIn text post with more depth
  and a professional frame. Same insight, different packaging.
- IG Reel / short → upload **natively** to LinkedIn (captioned, vertical or square) with a
  strong text hook above it. Never just a link to IG/YouTube.

**Atomize 1 long asset → a week of posts:**

| Source (1 YouTube update or a week of IG stories) | LinkedIn outputs |
|---|---|
| The core idea | 1 text breakdown (Mon/Wed) |
| The key points | 1 document carousel (System Breakdown) |
| Best 30–90s moments | 1–2 native clips (captioned) |
| A sharp line | 1 quote post |
| Any data shown | 1 "The Tape" numbers post |

So **one source asset = 5–7 LinkedIn pieces.** A week of content from one batching session.
(LinkedIn only — we are not running TikTok.)

**The marketing person's weekly batching workflow:**
1. **Mon (1–2 h):** pick the week's source asset(s). Cut clips, write all 5–6 posts, build the
   carousel. Schedule everything.
2. **Daily (15–20 min):** the engagement routine (§6) — non-negotiable, this is where reach is
   won.
3. **Fri (15 min):** check the KPIs (§8), note the best performer, plan next week's source.

Keep a simple content log (one sheet): date · format · pillar · hook · CTA · link · result.

## 5. Sales Navigator — the targeting + outbound engine (where scale comes from)

Content brings inbound. Sales Navigator + the AI bring **predictable, scalable outbound.**
This is the second engine, and it's where "maximum leads" really lives.

**Build the ICP target lists (mirror icp.md):**
- Filters: **seniority** = senior / manager / director / owner; **industries** =
  tech/software, consulting, medical, legal, engineering, non-crypto founders (i.e.
  *non-finance professionals with capital*); **geography** = English-speaking markets;
  **exclude** finance/banking, "trader" titles, and obvious crypto-natives.
- Save these as Lead Lists / saved searches. These become the AI's cold-outreach list
  (Trigger A).

**The warm-outbound sequence (NOT spray-and-pray — this protects the account and converts):**
1. **Warm them first.** Before any DM: view their profile, follow, and ideally engage with one
   of their posts. Now the CGP profile isn't a stranger.
2. **They also see Crypto Gameplan's content** (profile view → they check the feed → the posts do the
   selling passively). This is the content × outbound flywheel.
3. **Then the connect / opener** → the AI takes the conversation from here (the cold opener,
   then the 4-question screen).

**Two warm lists the AI should prioritize over pure cold:**
- **Post engagers** — anyone who comments on / reacts to Crypto Gameplan's content (Trigger C). Warmest
  inbound-ish leads.
- **Profile viewers** — Sales Nav shows who viewed the CGP profile. Intent signal → the AI
  reaches out.

**Scale + safety:** Sales Nav supplies the *volume* of targeted leads; the AI supplies the
*conversation capacity* humans don't have; LinkedIn's daily action limits cap the throughput.
So we **ramp volume slowly** as the account warms (per the founder's steer: start low, scale what's safe).
The AI's send limits + randomization are the guardrail — never blast.

## 5b. Cold start + network growth (the reach foundation)

Reach is capped by who's in Crypto Gameplan's network. With a small or generic network, even great
posts reach almost no one. So before reach can compound, we build the right network. This is
the most important early-stage work and the part most plans skip.

**Solving the cold start (weeks 1–4):**
1. **Engagement-first, not posting-first.** Spend the first 2 weeks commenting (thoughtfully)
   on 10–20 posts/day from creators the ICP already follows. People discover Crypto Gameplan through his
   comments before his posts — this seeds the first audience.
2. **Seed the first-hour engagement.** Line up a small group (Jacques, the team, a few allies)
   to like + comment in the **first 15 minutes** of each post. Early velocity is what triggers
   the algorithm to expand reach. This is white-hat and standard practice.
3. **Port the existing audience.** Crypto Gameplan already has an audience on Instagram / YouTube. Tell
   them about the new LinkedIn presence (story, bio link, a pinned post) so warm followers seed the base.
4. **Optimize for the "see more" + comment** on every post from day one (dwell + comments are
   the only signals that matter when the network is small).

**Network growth as an ongoing reach lever:**
- Send **targeted connection requests daily** to the Sales Nav ICP (start ~15–20/day, ramp as
  the account warms — never blast, account safety = revenue continuity). Light personal note,
  no pitch.
- Every accepted connection is one more person who sees every future post. The first ~1,000
  right-fit connections turn a dead feed into a compounding one.
- Accept + engage with right-fit inbound connection requests (the content will start
  generating these).
- This network-building is also the **outbound list** (§5) and the **warm audience** (§6) —
  same action, three payoffs.

> Practical sequencing: weeks 1–2 are mostly engagement + connections + profile (low posting),
> weeks 3–4 ramp the content rhythm onto a network that can now actually see it.

## 6. The community + engagement engine (organic feel, real reach)

Reach is earned in the comments as much as in the posts. 15–20 min/day, right after posting:

- **Reply to every comment on Crypto Gameplan's posts** in the first hour (each reply is a reach signal
  and starts a relationship).
- **Comment thoughtfully on 5–10 posts** from adjacent voices the ICP follows (serious
  analysts, finance-adjacent creators, fintech founders). Add a real point — never "great
  post". This borrows their audience and builds Crypto Gameplan's name in the right rooms.
- **Engage with the Sales Nav target list's content** (warming, §5).
- **Make it a two-way thing:** run polls, ask "what's your take?", feature good community
  answers in a follow-up post ("a few of you said X — here's how I'd frame it"). People follow
  creators who make them feel part of something. That's the community, and it's also reach.

This engagement routine is what turns followers into a community and a cold network into a
warm one. It is not optional — it's half the system.

## 7. The funnel: every reach activity routes into the AI

Nothing is "just content." Every top-of-funnel action has a path into the screening AI:

| Source | AI trigger | Path |
|---|---|---|
| Post says "DM me 'system'" | **Trigger B** | warm DM → walkthrough → 4-question screen |
| Someone comments / engages | **Trigger C** | AI DMs them, references the post |
| Sales Nav cold list | **Trigger A** | AI cold opener → screen |
| Lead-magnet quiz completion | **Trigger D** | AI bridges to the screen |

All four converge on: **screen → qualified → book the call** (the AI handles it; unqualified
get answers stored + a graceful exit, no call). Content + outbound are the fuel; the AI is the
engine that turns volume into booked calls without a human in every DM.

| Layer | Metric | Why |

**The loop:** each week, double down on the pillar + format combos that produce DMs and
profile views; cut what only earns vanity likes. Feed the winners back into the batching
session. The system gets sharper every week.

## 9. Compliance — the hard guardrails (share with the marketer, no exceptions)

The founder is **not a licensed financial advisor**, and public content carries more liability than
private DMs. Never:

- Quote performance, returns, % gains, or member counts ("up 30%", "beat BTC", "X members").
- Predict price or direction ("BTC will hit $X", "alts are about to run", "sell now"). CGP's
  entire premise is we follow flow, we don't predict — predictions break the brand AND the
  compliance line. (Especially watch this on "The Tape" numbers post: report the **fact**, not
  a **call**.)
- Frame anything as personal financial advice ("you should buy X"). Use "the system reads…",
  "a systematic investor would frame this as…".
- Promise outcomes ("guaranteed", "risk-free", "consistent returns", "life-changing").
- Use crypto-bro language (ape, diamond hands, HODL, to the moon, WAGMI) — wrong audience,
  wrong register.

**Voice:** calm specialist, prestige over loudness. If a post sounds like an influencer trying
to pump, rewrite it. When in doubt on a market take, describe the **framework**, not a **call**.

## 11. The 90-day ramp (phased — don't do everything day one)

**Phase 1 — Foundation (weeks 1–4):** profile overhaul (§2), set up the recurring formats,
start the daily content rhythm from the repurposing engine, build the daily engagement habit.
Soft comment-CTAs only. Goal: establish "Crypto Gameplan = the systematic one" + warm up the account.

**Phase 2 — Outbound on (weeks 5–8):** build the Sales Nav lists, start warm outreach feeding
the AI (Trigger A) at low, safe volume. Layer in the first hard CTAs. Goal: add a predictable
second lead source on top of inbound.

**Phase 3 — Scale + optimize (weeks 9–12):** double down on the winning content formats, grow
outbound volume as the account proves safe, lean into the warm lists (engagers + profile
viewers). Goal: a compounding engine producing a steady, growing flow of qualified calls.

After 90 days: keep the rhythm, feed the repurposing engine weekly, let Sales Nav + the AI run
the outbound, and review the loop (§8) every week. Reach compounds; the AI scales the
conversations; the calls fill the calendar.
